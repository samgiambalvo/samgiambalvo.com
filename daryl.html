Where's Daryl is a gun-violence prevention toolkit that needed a website to scale nationally.

Overview

In partnership with the Los Angeles Unified School District, this studio class – a continuation of the “Where’s Daryl?” educational toolkit created in 2012 – challenged students to develop an online teacher training portal so the anti-gun violence curriculum could be scaled nationally and reach a wider potential audience of users.

My group researched best practices for educational teaching, and interviewed district administrators and health program developers before testing our hypotheses with local middle school teachers.

Preparing For Interviews

Before interviewing LAUSD adminisitrator Tim Kordic and toolkit designer Maria Moon, we read whitepapers and gathered secondary research on middle school teachers in the US to ensure that we could ask the right questions.

Timothy Kordic is the director of Health Education Programs at the Los Angeles Unified School District. He provided insight on the need of the school district to evaluate the Where's Daryl program for both students and teachers from the same website.

Designer Maria Moon was part of the original Where's Daryl project. She explained how important the quality of the physical materials proved to be for teachers and students.

Personas & Insight

Our conversations allowed us to identify all potential visitors to the Where's Daryl site and their corresponding needs. We learned that teachers work in different ways. Some will teach this toolkit the first week of September all the way through while others break the lessons up over the course of a few months. They need a way to build custom lesson plans based on their availability.

Sketching & Prototyping

Our goal was to introduce the Where’s Daryl project to teachers in a concise but informative way, and to allow them to customize their lesson plan. In addition, allow funders and visitors to learn and donate, and allow district administrators to receive feedback.

We sketched initial wireframes, and designed low and mid fidelity mockups in preparation of testing.

Preparing To Test

We scheduled a time to test our prototypes with a group of teachers at Visa Middle School in Los Angeles. We came up with a series of questions about class size, the use of technology, curriculum planning and the pros and cons of the existing Where's Daryl toolkit.

Synthesis & Iteration

Our key insight was that we were overprioritizing the needs of secondary personas; teachers were overwhelmed by the options above the fold and didn't need background information on the program. To adjust, we reduced the number of call-to-actions above the fold, condensed the info, and made a number of other changes.

We designed a series of supporting pages that allow teachers to build individual lesson plans based on their schedules.

Developing a Prototype

I worked with my teammate Linh Ho to develop a coded prototype of the site to present to LAUSD admnisitrators at the end of the term.
