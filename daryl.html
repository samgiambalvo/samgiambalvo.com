Where's Daryl is a gun-violence prevention toolkit that needed a website to scale nationally.

Overview

In partnership with the Los Angeles Unified School District, this studio class – a continuation of the “Where’s Daryl?” educational toolkit created in 2012 – challenged students to develop an online teacher training portal so the anti-gun violence curriculum could be scaled nationally and reach a wider potential audience of users.

We researched best practices for educational teaching, and interviewed district administrators, health program developers before testing our hypotheses with local middle school teachers.

Timothy Kordic is the director of Health Education Programs at the Los Angeles Unified School District. He provided insight on the need of the school district to evaluate the Where's Daryl program for both students and teachers from the same website.

Designer Maria Moon was part of the original Where's Daryl project. She explained how important the quality of the physical materials proved to be for teachers and students.

Identifying Personas

We spoke with teachers from Vista Middle School, LAUSD administrators and potential fundraisers in order to identify all possible visitors to the Where's Daryl site.

Focus

After charting the needs of our personas, we decided to prioritize the teachers - both new and experienced.

Prototyping

We designed the initial wireframes and used InVision to test medium-fidelity mockups with teachers at Vista Middle School.

Testing

We brought our prototypes to the teachers and received feedback that informed our final iteration.

Synthesis

We learned that we were overprioritizing the needs of secondary personas. Teachers were overwhelmed by the options above the fold and didn't need background information on the program.

Implementation

We designed and developed a live prototype of the final iteration that was presented to LAUSD administrators.

Structuring The Lessons

We designed a series of supporting pages that allow teachers to build individual lesson plans based on their schedules.

Sam Giambalvo is a designer and developer solving digital and physical problems.
