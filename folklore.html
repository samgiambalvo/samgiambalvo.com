Folkore is an AR app for Instrument employees to discover stories about their colleagues.

Overview

In 2018, Instrument partnered with Google Cloud and The New York Times to create an immersive experience that used AR to tell untold stories within historic photos. The development team had invested a considerable amount of time into image detection and web-based augmented reality technologies, and was itching to build something new with the same tools.

Working with a small team of designers and developers, I was tasked with reframing the New York Times experience as an internal project that told personal stories of Instrument employees and involved the creation of an entirely new brand, user flow and live product.

The final experience

The final product was a web app that detected photos of Instrument employees from a permanent wall installation and shared personal stories written by members of the team.

Building the brand

This technology felt like magic - I wanted the brand to reflect that. Through typography, iconography, and motion, I tried to make Folklore feel playful but intelligent.

Designing the palette

To give stories an element of personalization, we derived a unique palette of colors from each photo that was applied to the layouts.

Continuing the story

To allow people to read the stories from anywhere, we built a desktop site that mirrored the mobile experience.
